Bill to help Taiwan regain WHO status passes Congress, sent to Biden for signature WASHINGTON, April 27. The U.S. House of Representatives unanimously passed legislation on Wednesday calling on the State Department to submit a plan to help Taiwan regain its observer status at the World Health Organization, seeking to boost the island as it faces pressure from China. The House passed the bill 425 to 0, sending it to the White House because it passed the Senate in August.
 
当地时间4月27日，美国众议院以425票对0票、一致通过法案，指示国务卿制定计划，以恢复台湾在世卫大会的观察员地位。由于法案已在去年8月获参议院通过，因此预计接下来将送交拜登总统签署后生效。 由于中共的反对，台湾被排除在世界卫生组织（World Health Organization，WHO）等大多数全球组织以外。自俄罗斯入侵乌克兰以来，出于对中共可能采取侵台行动的考量，台湾的警戒级别也随之上升，类似的关切有助于推动美国支持台湾。
